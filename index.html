Nokia E72

Discover the legendary QWERTY smartphone of the Nokia E-series lineup.

Mastering Mobility in the Corporate Age

Launched in summer 2009, Nokia E72 wasn't just another phone — it was the definitive mobile office tool for the discerning business professional. This sleek, business-oriented smartphone ran the robust Symbian OS and defined the gold standard for mobile QWERTY efficiency.

Key Innovations That Defined Excellence

E72 built upon the massive success of its predecessor E71 by significantly boosting performance and features:

  • Blazing Speed Upgrade: It featured a new, faster 600 MHz ARM processor resulting in a noticeably smoother, more responsive user experience across all applications.
  • Next-Level Navigation: Introduced the revolutionary Optical Navi Key. This small, touch-sensitive square allowed users to scroll through long emails, documents and the web browser with the swipe of a thumb, enhancing one-handed usability.
  • Enhanced Imaging:The device delivered a worthy camera upgrade, featuring a 5-megapixel auto-focusing camera with an LED flash.
  • Smarter Software:E72 introduced integrated instant messaging right from the home screen and came pre-loaded with the latest version of Ovi Maps, offering turn-by-turn A-GPS navigation and a built-in magnetic compass.

The Responsive Business Edge

As Nokia Vice President Ukko Lappalainen highlighted upon its debut, E72 was engineered for a world increasingly reliant on instant communication:

Research shows that people are spending an average of five full days per year responding to emails. Nokia E72 was designed exactly with this kind of person in mind — it allows the owner to be more responsive and communicate how they want, whenever they want and wherever they are.

E72 delivered this responsiveness with dedicated, switchable profiles for work and personal use, allowing users to seamlessly transition between their professional and private lives without distraction. It wasn't just a communication tool; it was an organisational powerhouse, solidifying Nokia's status as the leader of enterprise mobility.
