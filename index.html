Nokia E72

Discover the legendary QWERTY smartphone of the Nokia E-series lineup.

Mastering Mobility in the Corporate Age

Launched on 15 June 2009, at the high-profile Nokia Connections event in Singapore, the Nokia E72 wasn't just another phone—it was the definitive mobile office tool, a powerful evolution of the beloved Eseries designed for the discerning business professional. This sleek, business-oriented "candybar" smartphone ran the robust Symbian OS and defined the gold standard for mobile QWERTY efficiency.

Key Innovations That Defined Excellence

The E72 built upon the massive success of its predecessor, the E71, by significantly boosting performance and features:

  • Blazing Speed Upgrade: It featured a new, faster 600 MHz ARM processor (up from 369 MHz in the E71), resulting in a noticeably smoother, more responsive user experience across all applications.
  • Next-Level Navigation: Introducing the revolutionary Optical Navi Key. This small, touch-sensitive square replaced the standard D-pad's center button, allowing users to scroll through long emails, documents, the web browser, and photo galleries with the swipe of a thumb, dramatically enhancing one-handed usability.
  • Enhanced Imaging:The device finally delivered a worthy camera upgrade, featuring a 5-megapixel auto-focusing camera with an LED flash, far surpassing the E71's fixed-focus 3.2MP camera.
  • Smarter Software:The E72 introduced integrated instant messaging right from the home screen and came pre-loaded with the latest version of Ovi Maps, offering turn-by-turn A-GPS navigation and a built-in magnetic compass.

The Responsive Business Edge

As Nokia Vice President Ukko Lappalainen highlighted upon its debut, the E72 was engineered for a world increasingly reliant on instant communication:

Research shows that people are spending an average of five full days per year... responding to emails. The Nokia E72 was designed exactly with this kind of person in mind—it allows the owner to be more responsive and communicate how they want, whenever they want and wherever they are.

The E72 delivered this responsiveness with dedicated, switchable profiles for work and personal use, allowing users to seamlessly transition between their professional and private lives without distraction. It wasn't just a communication tool; it was an organisational powerhouse, solidifying Nokia's status as the king of enterprise mobility.
